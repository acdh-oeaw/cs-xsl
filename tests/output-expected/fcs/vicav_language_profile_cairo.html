General

The Egyptian varieties of Arabic form a quite diversified linguistic group. They are usually subdivided into four major subgroups: the dialects of Upper Egypt, Middle Egypt, Lower Egypt and those spoken in the Oases. The dialect of the capital belongs to the northern group. The first more detailed description of Cairo Arabic was published as early as 1880 by Wilhelm Spitta.

By contrast to most other varieties of vernacular Arabic, the dialect of Cairo has been reduced to writing as well. There exists quite a significant amount of literary output, in particular stage plays.
